What is TEASA?
An interactive, secure, cloud-based application built for patients and their caregivers.

How does TEASA help patients with their healthcare journey?
TEASA enables patients and their caregivers to navigate through the complex healthcare system. TEASA lets patients create their own care teams and grant secure access to their healthcare information, schedules, and tasks. TEASA connects patients and their caregivers with experienced personal advocates to help navigate any barriers to achieving the health and wellness goals that matter most.

Data Analytics Platform

The Livanta™ Data Analytics Platform (LDAP) is an integrated collection of tools that allows Livanta to mine, assess, infer, and report on a multitude of data sources. Livanta leverages a wide array of data sources from organizations, such as the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS), state departments of health, and commercial data repositories to establish dynamic data warehouses that offer unique insights into healthcare. Livanta uses artificial intelligence, machine learning, and conventional tools to assemble and generate tangible findings quickly.

CompassWeb

Livanta developed the CompassWeb application to support the Medicare Beneficiary and Family Centered Care-Quality Improvement Organization (BFCC-QIO) contract to perform reviews of Medicare beneficiary appeals and complaints about healthcare services, as well as reviews of the quality of care and Medicare providers. CompassWeb collects and maintains information about beneficiaries, as well as entities providing healthcare services. CompassWeb records and shares notes from Livanta staff and remote physician reviewers. This application can also compose, send, and store decision letters to providers and beneficiaries and run reports on statistical and other management data.

Robotic Process Automation

Livanta™ Robotic Process Automation (RPA) technology consists of application “robots” that automate specific tasks that would normally require manual interaction with web-based applications. Livanta’s RPA applications integrate directly with a web browser and add functionality to existing web-based applications to automate repetitive tasks as well as provide additional custom functionality that is not currently present in the web application.

Trustfolio™ ERP

Trustfolio™ Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) system consists of a tightly integrated portfolio of software applications hosted in a central environment, using a central database, and designed to automate business processes. The business applications portfolio includes human resources, accounting, proposal pricing, project budgeting, contract management, and analytics and reporting.

The Trustfolio™ ERP includes two-way integration with QuickBooks using a middleware component called the Accounting System Broker (ASB). The ASB currently supports QuickBooks and is designed to connect to additional accounting systems in the future. Trustfolio™ uses the Workuments software applications to support human resources functions.

Case Management

Livanta’s Case Management System (LCMS), part of the Zuilder suite of products, is used for all software projects at Livanta. LCMS is a low-code/no-code platform limiting development to only what is needed to support the customer’s data requirements, workflows, user roles, and specific forms and reports. LCMS is configured for several health and human services use cases and includes a myriad of features and functions.

The system has dashboards configurable at both user and management levels. User dashboards, such as the one pictured above, provide task- and schedule-based information to assist with daily work. Users can configure management-level dashboards to support trending and demographic analysis across multiple business units.

The system has configurable workflows to support case management activities, including intake and investigation workflows. These workflows incorporate Artificial Intelligence through IBM Watson to identify abuse allegations from an intake narrative. The system supports forms automation. These forms can include context-sensitive sections, in which additional parts of the form are presented based upon the answers to questions within the form.
